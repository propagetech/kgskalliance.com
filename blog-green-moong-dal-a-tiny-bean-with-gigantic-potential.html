Green Moong Dal: A Tiny Bean with Gigantic Potential

Green moong dal, the unassuming champion of pulses, isn't just packed with nutrition, it's a global phenomenon waiting to be explored! From its humble origins to its ever-growing popularity, let's embark on a journey with KGSK Alliance Pvt Ltd to unlock the potential of this mighty tiny bean.
Product Info:
  • Form: Available whole, split, husked, ground, and sprouted.
  • Taste: Mild, earthy, slightly sweet.
  • Texture: Varies depending on processing (firm when whole, creamy when dal).
  • Species: Vigna radiata.
  • Family: Fabaceae (Legume).
  • Origin: Southeast Asia, likely India.
  • Nature: Tropical, warm-season annual legume.
  • Growing Cycle: Short, around 60-80 days.
  • Life Span/Storage: Whole moong dal can be stored for up to 1-year, split dals for 6 months, and sprouted for 3-5 days.
Benefits & Demand :
Nutrition: Rich in protein, fibre, vitamins, and minerals.
Health Benefits: Aids digestion, lowers cholesterol, supports heart health, and more.
Increasing Demand: Global demand for pulses, including green moong, is projected to reach $62.8 billion by 2027 due to rising health awareness and plant-based trends.
A Closer Look: Nutritional Breakdown of One Green Moong Dal:

  • Protein: 2.2g
  • Fiber: 2.0g
  • Iron: 2% Daily Value
